What is “Fixed Fee” Conveyancing?

When buying or selling property, understanding the costs involved is just as important as understanding the legal process. One of the most common terms you’ll hear when engaging a conveyancer is “fixed fee conveyancing.” But what does that actually mean — and how does it compare to traditional hourly billing or variable pricing structures?

In essence, fixed fee conveyancing is a transparent pricing model in which the conveyancer charges a single, predetermined fee for their professional services. This approach eliminates uncertainty, allowing clients to budget confidently without worrying about hidden charges or fluctuating costs.

This article breaks down how fixed fee conveyancing works, what it typically includes, the benefits and potential limitations, and how to ensure you get true value for money. We’ll also highlight how experienced professionals like First Class Legal use this model to provide clarity, fairness, and peace of mind for their clients.

1. Understanding Conveyancing Costs

Conveyancing involves a range of legal, administrative, and compliance tasks necessary to transfer ownership of property. The cost reflects the time, expertise, and risk involved in ensuring that every step complies with the law.

Traditionally, some firms charged on an hourly basis or applied variable pricing depending on the complexity of the transaction. However, this could make it difficult for clients to estimate total expenses, especially if unexpected issues arose during the process.

Fixed fee conveyancing was developed as a response to these concerns, offering more predictable pricing and simpler communication around costs.

2. What Does “Fixed Fee” Really Mean?

When a conveyancer offers a fixed fee, they are committing to a total cost that covers all standard conveyancing services for your transaction type — whether you are buying, selling, or transferring property. This price remains unchanged, provided the transaction stays within the agreed scope of work.

Typical inclusions under a fixed fee arrangement are:

  • Preparation or review of the contract of sale and disclosure documents.
  • Conducting title and property searches.
  • Preparing transfer documents.
  • Calculating and managing settlement adjustments.
  • Coordinating with your lender, real estate agent, and the other party’s conveyancer.
  • Arranging and attending settlement (including electronic settlements through PEXA).
  • Post-settlement notifications to authorities.

However, it’s important to note that some disbursements — such as government fees, search costs, or stamp duty — are usually excluded from the fixed professional fee, since they are external costs paid to third parties.

5. What’s Included in a Fixed Fee Package

While details vary between firms, a typical fixed fee conveyancing package includes:

  • Client consultation and file setup.
  • Contract review or preparation.
  • Title search and property history checks.
  • Communication with relevant authorities and banks.
  • Preparation and lodgement of transfer documents.
  • Settlement coordination and completion.
  • Advice on legal obligations and timelines.

The conveyancer’s quote will also usually outline “disbursements” such as title office fees, rate certificates, and verification of identity (VOI) costs, which are separate but disclosed clearly from the outset.

6. The Benefits of Fixed Fee Conveyancing

  • Financial Certainty: You’ll know your costs before committing, which helps with budgeting, especially when buying property where cash flow matters.
  • Transparency and Trust: Clients can clearly see what services they are paying for.
  • No Surprise Bills: You avoid unexpected “extras” that might arise from hourly billing.
  • Streamlined Communication: The focus shifts from billing hours to providing value.
  • Efficiency: Conveyancers are motivated to deliver efficient, high-quality work within the agreed scope.

7. Are There Any Limitations?

Fixed fee conveyancing works best for standard transactions, but there are cases where additional costs might still apply. These are usually for unexpected complications such as:

  • Complex title or boundary disputes.
  • Lost or missing title documents.
  • Delays caused by third parties, such as lenders or developers.
  • Renegotiations or contract variations after signing.
  • Dealing with deceased estates, trusts, or company-owned properties.

A transparent conveyancer will always outline these potential “out of scope” situations upfront, so you understand when extra costs might arise and why.

  • Whether the service includes contract review before signing.
  • Online reviews or testimonials from past clients.

9. The Technology and Process Efficiency Advantage

Fixed fee conveyancing is often paired with technology-driven systems that improve efficiency. Many firms now use digital settlement platforms such as PEXA (Property Exchange Australia), allowing electronic document lodgement and faster settlements.

These innovations reduce manual paperwork, cut down on delays, and enhance client transparency — all while keeping costs stable within the fixed fee model.

10. Legal and Professional Safeguards

Even under a fixed fee arrangement, clients are fully protected by professional standards and insurance. Licensed conveyancers are regulated under state law and must carry professional indemnity insurance to safeguard clients from loss due to error or negligence.

This means you can enjoy predictable pricing without compromising on legal protection or service quality.
